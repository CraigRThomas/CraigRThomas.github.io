Personal Projects

Pluto's Revenge (2015)

Unity/C#
Pluto’s Revenge is a simple action game where the player plays as as anthropormorphized Pluto, who has just discovered that they’ve been declassified as a planet. Since one of the criteria preventing Pluto from being classified as a planet is the amount of debris in its orbital path, Pluto goes on a rampage to try to clear that issue up. Players fling space rocks at the debris, breaking it up over time until encountering one of the scientists responsible for Pluto’s demotion: Neil deGrasse Tyson. Rather than fighting Pluto, however, he patiently explains the demotion, and eventually makes Pluto (and hopefully the player) understand the decision.

Pluto’s Revenge was developed as part of the 2015 ROM Game Jam, hosted by the Royal Ontario Museum. The museum allowed the participating teams access to their extensive exhibits and enthusiastic experts, and prompted us to make a game that examined a topic concerning “Space Rocks”. Considering the information New Horizons had been beaming back to us at the time, my team and I decided Pluto would be an interesting topic to tackle. The ROM graciously supported the games following the jam, inviting us to playtesting sessions hosted as an exhibit in the museum, which helped to refine and polish the games.

Role: Sole Developer (With 3 Artists)

EMDash (2014)

Unity/C#
Top-down 3D co-op arena wave-clearing game. 2 players must work together to clear the waves of enemies that spawn. Players can move freely, but the only way to attack is to charge a dash attack that always aims you towards your partner. While you are charging your attack, your partner’s movement speed is decreased, meaning communication is vital to success. Made for TOJAM9 with 4 other team members (2 other programmers and 2 artists).
